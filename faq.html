Frequently Asked Questions

Do you create custom gowns?

Yes. All Brides in Black gowns can be customized to match each bride's vision. We work closely with our clients to modify silhouettes, fabrics, lace details, and finishing elements to create a truly unique gown.

How long does it take to create a custom gown?

Most custom gowns require between 8–12 weeks to complete depending on the complexity of the design. We recommend beginning the design process at least three to four months before your wedding date.

Do you offer ready‑made gowns?

Yes. Our collections include both ready‑made designs and custom pieces. Ready‑made gowns can be adjusted for fit while custom gowns are designed from scratch to match your vision.

Do you ship internationally?

Yes. Brides in Black proudly serves clients worldwide. International orders are carefully packaged and shipped with secure tracking to ensure your gown arrives safely.

How do I schedule a consultation?

You can schedule a consultation directly through our contact form on the website. During the consultation we will discuss your design ideas, preferred silhouette, fabrics, and timeline.

Can gowns be altered after purchase?

Yes. Minor alterations such as adjusting the length, straps, or waist can be completed to ensure a perfect fit. We recommend scheduling alterations several weeks before the wedding.

What fabrics do you use?

Our gowns are crafted using premium materials including silk satin, velvet, layered tulle, lace, and chiffon. Every fabric is selected for quality, comfort, and visual elegance.

Do you offer veils or accessories?

Yes. Brides can request matching veils, gloves, lace capes, or custom accessories to complement their gown and create a complete bridal look.

What sizes do you offer?

We create gowns for all body types and sizes. Because many of our gowns are custom made, we can tailor the design to fit the bride perfectly.

Can I request design changes to a gown from the collection?

Absolutely. Brides can request modifications such as adding sleeves, changing the neckline, adjusting the train length, or selecting different fabrics to personalize their gown.
